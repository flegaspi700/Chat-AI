=== PASTA CARBONARA ===

Ingredients:
- 400g spaghetti
- 200g pancetta or guanciale, diced
- 4 large eggs
- 100g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
- 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
- Black pepper, freshly ground
- Salt for pasta water

Instructions:
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti according to package directions until al dente.
2. While pasta cooks, fry pancetta in a large skillet over medium heat until crispy (about 8-10 minutes).
3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Pecorino, Parmesan, and plenty of black pepper.
4. Reserve 1 cup pasta water, then drain pasta.
5. Remove pancetta from heat. Add hot pasta to the skillet and toss.
6. Remove from heat completely. Quickly stir in egg mixture, adding pasta water gradually until creamy.
7. Serve immediately with extra cheese and pepper.

Tips: The key is to remove from heat before adding eggs to prevent scrambling. The residual heat will cook them perfectly.

=== CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIES ===

Ingredients:
- 2¼ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 cup butter, softened
- ¾ cup granulated sugar
- ¾ cup packed brown sugar
- 2 large eggs
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 2 cups chocolate chips
- 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:
1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
2. Mix flour, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.
3. Beat butter and both sugars until creamy (about 3 minutes).
4. Add eggs and vanilla, beat well.
5. Gradually blend in flour mixture.
6. Fold in chocolate chips and nuts.
7. Drop rounded tablespoons onto ungreased cookie sheets.
8. Bake 9-11 minutes until golden brown.
9. Cool on baking sheet for 2 minutes, then transfer to wire rack.

Makes: About 5 dozen cookies

=== THAI GREEN CURRY ===

Ingredients:
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 3 tbsp green curry paste
- 400ml coconut milk
- 2 cups chicken stock
- 500g chicken breast, sliced
- 1 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 200g bamboo shoots
- 100g Thai basil leaves
- 2 kaffir lime leaves
- 2 red chilies, sliced
- Jasmine rice for serving

Instructions:
1. Heat oil in a wok or large pan over medium-high heat.
2. Fry curry paste for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
3. Add thick coconut cream (from top of can), stir until oil splits.
4. Add chicken, cook until no longer pink.
5. Pour in remaining coconut milk and stock.
6. Add fish sauce, sugar, and lime leaves. Simmer 10 minutes.
7. Add bamboo shoots, cook 5 minutes.
8. Stir in basil and chilies just before serving.
9. Serve over jasmine rice.

Spice level: Adjust curry paste amount to taste. Start with 2 tbsp for mild.

=== CLASSIC CAESAR SALAD ===

For the Dressing:
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 anchovy fillets
- 2 egg yolks
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- ¾ cup olive oil
- ½ cup Parmesan, grated
- Salt and black pepper

For the Salad:
- 2 heads romaine lettuce, chopped
- 1 cup croutons
- ½ cup Parmesan shavings
- Extra black pepper

Dressing Instructions:
1. Mash garlic and anchovies into a paste.
2. Whisk in egg yolks, lemon juice, and mustard.
3. Slowly drizzle in olive oil while whisking continuously.
4. Stir in grated Parmesan. Season to taste.

Assembly:
1. Toss lettuce with dressing until well coated.
2. Top with croutons and Parmesan shavings.
3. Finish with freshly ground black pepper.

Serves: 4 as a side, 2 as a main

=== BEEF TACOS ===

Ingredients:
- 1 lb ground beef (80/20)
- 1 onion, diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp paprika
- ½ tsp oregano
- Salt and pepper to taste
- ½ cup water
- 8 taco shells or tortillas

Toppings:
- Shredded lettuce
- Diced tomatoes
- Shredded cheese
- Sour cream
- Salsa
- Guacamole
- Fresh cilantro
- Lime wedges

Instructions:
1. Brown ground beef in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Drain excess fat, leaving about 1 tablespoon.
3. Add onion and garlic, cook until softened (3-4 minutes).
4. Stir in all spices, cook 1 minute.
5. Add water, simmer 5-7 minutes until thickened.
6. Warm taco shells according to package directions.
7. Fill shells with beef mixture.
8. Top with desired toppings.

Pro tip: Make your own taco seasoning instead of store-bought for better flavor and less sodium.

Serves: 4 people (2 tacos each)
